How do we actually empower our teams and PODs?

We're asking teams to move fast, collaborate closely, and own their decisions — but without end-to-end ownership, that's just pressure without power. Here's what true empowerment looks like, where we are today, and what has to change.

✓ Empowered Team
"New user activation is too low. The POD owns figuring out why and fixing it."
  • ✓ Triad digs into where onboarding breaks down — together, from day one
  • ✓ Designer prototypes solutions while TL validates feasibility in parallel
  • ✓ Team ships, measures activation, and iterates based on real data
  • ✓ Stakeholders review the outcome, not the screens
✗ Feature Factory
"Build onboarding flow V2. Here's what to design. We need it done by Q3."
  • ✗ PM translates stakeholder decisions into tickets
  • ✗ Designer polishes specs they had no say in
  • ✗ TL estimates work they didn't scope
  • ✗ The POD is a faster way to execute someone else's ideas

We've changed how teams work — not who owns the outcome.

When senior stakeholders dictate features, scope, and design decisions, POD autonomy is an illusion — regardless of how fast the team moves. To fix this, we need to get three things right.

Ownership
Teams need a meaningful scope of responsibility. Too narrow kills motivation. Too broad overwhelms. The scope should connect clearly to a business outcome the team can measure.
In Motion
Autonomy
When given a problem, teams must have enough control to solve it their own way. Doesn't mean no dependencies — means not being told how before they start.
Blocked
Alignment
Team boundaries should track with the architecture and business model. High alignment means fewer dependencies, faster decisions, clearer connection to outcomes.

📖

Cagan's framing: if your teams are still judged on shipping features rather than achieving outcomes, the shift hasn't happened yet.

What we need to do differently

1
Stop assigning features, start assigning outcomes
Give PODs a business problem to solve, not a solution to build. Define success by the metric that moves, not the thing that ships.
2
Redefine what stakeholders own
Stakeholders own the "why" and the outcome. The POD owns the "how." Until that boundary is clear and respected, you have a feature factory with better meetings.
3
Make end-to-end ownership real
Each POD should be able to trace a direct line from their work to a business outcome. If they can't, the scope is wrong.
